# Week 41 · The shitty first draft is a contemplative practice — and liberation from skill anxiety is one of art's most important gifts

**Phase:** VI — The Wide Waters / The Making
**Constellation:** The Making
**Star:** Process over product

---

## Grounding: The tyranny of skill

The art world's emphasis on skill — technical mastery as the criterion for legitimacy, the fully realized work as the standard against which everything is measured — is one of the most effective mechanisms for excluding ordinary bodies from making. Skill anxiety — the fear that one is not good enough to make — keeps enormous numbers of people away from one of the most healing practices available to the human nervous system. Anne Lamott's 'shitty first draft,' Julia Cameron's 'morning pages,' Shaun McNiff's 'trust the process' — these are not merely pedagogical strategies but spiritual and political ones: the insistence that the threshold for legitimate making is lower than the institution has claimed.

**Key sentence:** Skill anxiety is not a personal inadequacy — it is the institution's product: the systematic exclusion of ordinary bodies from making by raising the threshold for legitimate participation to the level where only those with training, time, and resources can clear it.

---

## Ethics: Who has been told they cannot make

The judgment that certain bodies cannot make — or cannot make anything worth seeing — has been distributed along familiar lines: gender, race, class, disability, educational background, the accident of having been exposed to the 'right' influences. These judgments are not aesthetic; they are social. The person told from childhood that they are not artistic, not creative, not a maker — told this reliably enough that the judgment has become self-administered — has been subjected to a second-satan verdict on their creative being. The open studio practice is an act of theological resistance to this verdict: *you were always enough.*

**Key sentence:** The verdict that certain bodies cannot make is not an aesthetic judgment but a political one — a distribution of creative legitimacy along the same lines that second-satan systems use to distribute every other form of power.

---

## Emergence: The liberation that process-orientation offers

When the focus shifts from product to process — from what the work will eventually be to what the making is right now — something important happens: the criterion by which the practitioner can succeed changes. Success is now measured by the quality of presence brought to the making, not by the quality of the object produced. This shift is genuinely liberating for those whose bodies have been told they cannot produce acceptable objects — because presence is available to everyone, regardless of technical skill, and the quality of presence can be deepened by practice in ways that are genuinely accessible.

**Key sentence:** The shift from product to process changes what success looks like — from the quality of the object to the quality of presence — and this shift is liberation for every body that has been told its products are not acceptable.

## Art Practice Invitation

Make something badly, deliberately. Choose the medium you are worst at, or deliberately use the wrong tool. Make quickly and do not correct. Let the badness be the point. Notice what freedom becomes available when the standard is released.
