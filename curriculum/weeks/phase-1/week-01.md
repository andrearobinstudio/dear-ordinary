# Week 01 · The body is not where the spiritual path ends; it is where it begins

**Phase:** I — The Body's Wisdom  
**Constellation:** The Body's Wisdom  
**Star:** Sensing the body from within (Interoception)

---

## Grounding: What the tradition has done with the body

Western spiritual traditions have largely treated the body as the problem — the site of distraction, desire, and disorder that must be subdued, transcended, or at least managed on the way to something higher. The contemplative ideal has too often been a disembodied one: the mind lifted free of its material situation, the soul finally unencumbered. This is not merely a theological position. It is a posture that has consequences for actual bodies — particularly bodies that have already been told, by a hundred systems, that they are the wrong kind.

**Key sentence:** The tradition's suspicion of the body is not spiritually neutral — it re-enacts, in theological language, the same logic that second-satan systems use to police, diminish, and exclude.

---

## Ethics: Whose body gets to be the problem, and whose the proof?

The body-as-obstacle framework does not apply equally. The bodies most urgently required to transcend their materiality are always the bodies that dominant culture has already marked as excessive, insufficient, or wrong — queer bodies, racialized bodies, disabled bodies, fat bodies, bodies in chronic pain. Meanwhile, the bodies whose experience is taken as the spiritual norm are rarely named as bodies at all; they simply pass as "the human." To call the body the obstacle is to smuggle in a politics while claiming to do theology.

**Key sentence:** The question is never simply "the body" — it is always *which* body, whose body, and who gets to decide when a body is spiritual enough to count.

---

## Emergence: Interoception as the beginning of everything

Neuroscientist Anil Seth describes the self as "a controlled hallucination" built on the brain's continuous reading of its own internal state. Interoception — the perception of signals from within the body — is the biological root of self-awareness, empathy, and the capacity to recognize the human in another. You cannot reliably sense another person's inner life if you have been trained to ignore your own. The body is not the obstacle to contemplative life; it is its instrument, its site, its very medium.

**Key sentence:** Before any other contemplative practice becomes available, there is this: learning to sense yourself from within — and discovering that this sensing is already, always, a form of prayer.

## Art Practice Invitation

Spend twenty minutes making something in response to this question: *What does your body feel like from the inside right now?* Not what it looks like. Not what you wish it felt like. What it actually feels like — the particular weight, temperature, pressure, rhythm of it, today, in this light.

Any medium. No product required.
