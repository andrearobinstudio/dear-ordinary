# Week 48 · The art practice is not evidence for the thesis — it is the thesis practicing itself

**Phase:** VII — The Making — Deep Practice
**Constellation:** All eight constellations
**Star:** Art practice as thesis enactment

---

## Grounding: What practice-based research means

Practice-based research — the scholarly tradition in which the practitioner's own creative practice is itself a form of inquiry — has spent considerable energy establishing that making is a genuine form of knowledge production, not merely illustration or application of knowledge produced elsewhere. Robin Nelson's *Practice as Research in the Arts*, Estelle Barrett and Barbara Bolt's *Practice as Research*, and the wider field of arts-based research establish the theoretical ground. In this curriculum, the claim is specific: the art practice does not evidence the thesis's claims, it performs them — the making is the hypothesis being tested in real time.

**Key sentence:** Practice-based research is not a softer version of empirical research — it is a different epistemology, one that locates knowledge production in the making itself, and the art practice in this curriculum is the primary research site.

---

## Ethics: Who gets to call their practice research

The question of who gets to designate their practice as research — as knowledge production worthy of the academy's recognition — is a question of institutional power. The boundaries of what counts as legitimate research have consistently excluded forms of inquiry that cannot be quantified, that produce knowledge in imagistic or embodied rather than propositional form, that center the practitioner's own experience as data. Decolonial research methodologies, participatory action research, and arts-based research have all challenged these boundaries — and doing so from within the academy requires both the willingness to speak the institution's language and the willingness to exceed it.

**Key sentence:** The practitioner-researcher who insists that their making is research is not lowering the standard — they are expanding the definition of knowledge to include forms of knowing that the dominant epistemology has excluded, and this is a political act as well as a scholarly one.

---

## Emergence: The making as the primary research site

Week forty-eight's practical task is methodological: articulating, in writing, how the art practice functions as a research site in this thesis. Not describing what was made, but describing how the making produced knowledge — what questions emerged from the practice that could not have been formed without it, what the body found that the mind could not have found alone, what the image held that language could not yet say. This is the methodology section's most important contribution: documenting the epistemology of making.

**Key sentence:** Documenting how the making produced knowledge — what the body found that the mind could not, what the image held that language could not say — is the methodology section's most important task, and it requires both scholarly precision and genuine attention to the practice's actual operations.
