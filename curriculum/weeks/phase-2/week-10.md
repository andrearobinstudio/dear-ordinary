# Week 10 · Secure attachment is not the absence of rupture — it is the reliable experience of finding one's way back

**Phase:** II — The Hearthstone  
**Constellation:** The Hearthstone  
**Star:** Repair

---

## Grounding: What predicts security

Mary Main's Adult Attachment Interview research established something counterintuitive: what predicts secure attachment in children is not perfect parental attunement. It is the parents' capacity to return after rupture — to recognize the disconnection and move back toward connection. The turning back toward is the engine of real intimacy. Security is built not from the promise that nothing will go wrong, but from the demonstrated capacity to return when it does.

**Key sentence:** Security is built not from the absence of rupture but from the accumulated experience of repair — of someone, or some practice, or some community, reliably turning back toward.

---

## Ethics: Who has been denied reliable repair

Many queer people, many people of faith who came out and were rejected, many people from family systems that replicated institutional cruelty, have never experienced reliable repair from the institutions that wounded them. The church that condemned does not return and repair. The family that rejected does not turn back toward. This is not merely painful; it is developmentally consequential — it shapes what the nervous system expects from intimacy, from community, from God.

**Key sentence:** The absence of reliable repair from second-satan systems is not a neutral gap — it shapes what the nervous system learns to expect from every subsequent relationship, and this is among the most durable harms such systems inflict.

---

## Emergence: Repair as the engine of intimacy and practice

The studio, the contemplative practice, the therapeutic relationship — these become spaces of repair when they are reliably returned to after rupture. The consistency of returning matters more than the quality of any single session. The practice that can sustain itself through its own worst moments — through the weeks of bad work and blocked writing and skipped sessions — and return, is the practice doing what repair does.

**Key sentence:** The turning back toward — to the studio, to the practice, to the self, to the community — is not a recovery from failure but the very act that makes intimacy, healing, and creative depth possible.

## Art Practice Invitation

Return to something you abandoned — a piece, a practice, a medium. Not to fix it. To return to it. Notice what it is like to simply come back, without a plan to make it right.
