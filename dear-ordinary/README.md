## About This Project

**Dear Ordinary** is a 56-week curriculum for a thesis inquiry that moves between scholarly writing and visual art practice. The thesis queers the contemplative journey — refusing to map it as a linear path toward a destination, insisting instead that the "ordinary" body, previously policed and wounded by second-satan systems, can recognize itself as the primary site of the sacred.

The art practice does not illustrate the thesis. It acts on the same premise through different materials.

---

## Working Hypothesis

> By engaging in a non-linear art practice grounded in the *Path to Good* star chart, the "ordinary" body — previously policed and wounded by second-satan systems — can recognize itself as the primary site of the sacred, effectively queering the contemplative journey from a linear path of spiritual "progress" to a recursive process of recognition and somatic integration.

## Curriculum Structure

### The 56 Weeks: Eight Phases

The 56 weeks are organized into eight phases — one phase per constellation — but each phase draws on multiple constellations simultaneously. The curriculum spirals, not sequences.

| Phase | Weeks | Constellation Anchor | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | 1–7 | The Body's Wisdom | Foundations: the body as the site of inquiry |
| II | 8–14 | The Hearthstone | Ground: self-compassion as navigation |
| III | 15–21 | The Mystical Thread | Depth: via negativa and non-dual awareness |
| IV | 22–28 | The Engaged Path | Justice: liberation theology and lo cotidiano |
| V | 29–35 | The Wide Waters | Ecology: interbeing and the mystery held openly |
| VI | 36–42 | The Dark Night | Descent: grief as sacred work |
| VII | 43–49 | The Queer Path | Integration: exile, body theology, identity |
| VIII | 50–56 | The Making | Synthesis: art as contemplation, process as prayer |

## Weekly Structure

Every week follows four interlocking elements:

1. **Heading** — a provocation. A single statement or question that names the tension the week is working with. Not a topic label but an argument starter.
2. **Subheadings** — the three moves:
   - *Grounding*: what the system currently does or requires
   - *Ethics*: whose interests that serves and who it misses
   - *Emergence*: what becomes possible when the design is made adaptive and relational
3. **Keywords** — three to five conceptual anchors that connect this week to the broader star chart and recur across weeks in different combinations
4. **Key sentences** — one load-bearing claim per subheading, true at both the week's specific level and the level of the whole hypothesis

---

## The Three Axes

Every week moves across three dimensions simultaneously:

- **Inward ↔ Outward**: interior life in tension with relational and ecological justice
- **Dissolution ↔ Integration**: moving through dark night and grief toward wholeness
- **Embodied ↔ Transpersonal**: cellular somatic sensation in tension with the cosmic and mystical

---

## Art Practice Notes

Each week includes an art practice invitation. The practice does not illustrate the week's argument — it occupies the same territory from a different angle. Materials, process, and attention are as important as product (or more so).

The studio is a theological location.

## Primary Sources

The curriculum is grounded in the *Path to Good* star chart, a scholarly framework with anchors in:

- Polyvagal theory (Porges, Dana)
- Somatic trauma work (van der Kolk, Levine, Ogden)
- Contemplative neuroscience (Davidson, Lutz, Vessel)
- Self-compassion research (Neff, Coakley)
- Queer theology (Althaus-Reid, Cheng, Keller, Moore)
- Liberation theology (Gutiérrez, Cone, Isasi-Díaz, Williams)
- Mystical traditions (John of the Cross, Eckhart, Teresa of Ávila)
- Art therapy and expressive arts (McNiff, Kaimal, Levine S.K.)
- Ecological theology (Berry, Sheldrake, Thich Nhat Hanh)
