# Week 50 · The literature review is not a survey — it is an argument made from the archive

**Phase:** VIII — Synthesis
**Constellation:** Foundations / Literature
**Star:** Thesis structure: Literature

---

## Grounding: What the literature review actually does

The four-part thesis structure — Foundations, Literature, Investigation, Synthesis — assigns the literature review the task of demonstrating comprehensive knowledge of the field. But the most important function of a literature review is not comprehensive coverage; it is argument. The literature review shows not what has been said, but what has been said in relation to the thesis's specific claim. It shows the gap that the thesis fills, the conversation that the thesis enters, and the interlocutors with whom the thesis is in dialogue. A literature review that surveys without arguing is a display of knowledge; a literature review that argues is a contribution.

**Key sentence:** The literature review's most important function is not to demonstrate that you have read widely but to show which conversations your thesis enters, which gaps it fills, and which interlocutors it takes seriously — and this is an argument, not a survey.

---

## Ethics: Whose literature counts

The question of which bodies of literature are included in the review, and which are excluded, is a question of epistemological politics. A literature review on queering contemplation that draws only from white, Western, English-language sources — however comprehensive within those limits — has already made a choice that the thesis's content will need to reckon with. Liberation theology, mujerista theology, womanist theology, two-spirit scholarship, queer ecology — these are not supplementary to the main literature; they are part of what makes the thesis's argument possible, and their inclusion or exclusion defines the scope of the conversation the thesis enters.

**Key sentence:** The decision about which literature counts — whose scholarship is included, which traditions are cited, which voices are treated as interlocutors rather than data — is a political decision that shapes the thesis's epistemological commitments before a single argument is made.

---

## Emergence: Synthesizing across traditions

The particular challenge of this thesis's literature review is that it draws from at least eight distinct scholarly traditions — contemplative neuroscience, trauma studies, queer theology, liberation theology, expressive arts therapy, somatic psychology, ecological theology, and Celtic studies — and must synthesize across them without flattening any of them. The star chart's architecture is the guide: these traditions are related not by being versions of the same thing but by the particular cross-threaded relationships between them. The literature review must map those relationships, not merely list the traditions.

**Key sentence:** The literature review that synthesizes across the thesis's multiple scholarly traditions must map the relationships between those traditions — the cross-threaded connections that the star chart's architecture represents — rather than surveying each tradition separately.
