# Week 54 · The bibliography is not a list of sources — it is the map of the conversation the thesis entered

**Phase:** VIII — Synthesis
**Constellation:** Foundations
**Star:** Thesis structure: References and scholarly community

---

## Grounding: What the bibliography does

The bibliography of a thesis is not merely a list of sources consulted; it is the map of the scholarly community the thesis belongs to and the conversation it enters. The choices made in building the bibliography — which traditions are represented, which voices are cited, which languages are included — are scholarly and political choices. A bibliography that includes Althaus-Reid, Gutiérrez, Cone, Isasi-Díaz, and Nanda alongside Davidson, Porges, and Seth is a bibliography that is making an argument about the nature of the conversation this thesis enters — that it is interdisciplinary, cross-traditional, and deliberately non-homogeneous.

**Key sentence:** The bibliography is a political document as much as a scholarly one — the decision about whose work is cited, whose voices are treated as authoritative, and which traditions are included is a decision about whose knowledge counts.

---

## Ethics: Citation as care

The practice of citing accurately — giving credit to the scholars, practitioners, and communities whose work the thesis builds on — is a form of care and a form of justice. For traditions that have been historically undervalued — liberation theology, mujerista theology, two-spirit scholarship, queer ecology — citation is not merely academic courtesy; it is a small act of redistribution of scholarly recognition. The thesis that cites Isasi-Díaz as carefully as it cites Neff is practicing, in the most mundane scholarly form, the lo cotidiano theology it describes.

**Key sentence:** Citing the scholars whose work makes this thesis possible — particularly those from traditions that have been historically undervalued — is not academic courtesy but a small act of justice, a redistribution of scholarly recognition that the thesis's content demands.

---

## Emergence: The conversation that continues

A thesis's bibliography is also a forward-pointing document: the sources cited are the scholars with whom the field's future conversations will need to engage. Building a bibliography that includes voices the field has not yet sufficiently heard — that is genuinely interdisciplinary, genuinely cross-traditional, genuinely attentive to who has been missing from the conversation — is a contribution to the field's future as well as a record of the present inquiry.

**Key sentence:** A bibliography built with genuine attention to who has been missing from the conversation is not only a record of the thesis's sources — it is a proposal for what the field's future conversations should include.

## Art Practice Invitation

Review your full bibliography. Assess it: which traditions are most represented? Which are underrepresented? Which voices are missing that should be present? Identify three scholars whose work should be in the bibliography but is not yet, and begin engaging their work.
