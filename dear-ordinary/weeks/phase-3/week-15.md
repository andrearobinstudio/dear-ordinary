# Week 15 · Awareness prior to the subject-object split is not an achievement — it is what was there before the system taught you to divide

**Phase:** III — The Mystical Thread
**Constellation:** The Mystical Thread
**Star:** Non-dual awareness

---

## Grounding: The witness prior to grasping

Non-dual awareness — documented across Advaita Vedanta, Zen Buddhism, Sufism, and Christian mysticism — points to an awareness that precedes the split between self and world, subject and object. This is not a blank or empty state; it is the ground from which ordinary awareness arises. Neuroscientist Zoran Josipovic has identified distinctive neural signatures associated with non-dual awareness, suggesting it is a genuinely different mode of processing rather than merely a concept. It is not a state to achieve; it is what is always already present beneath the grasping.

**Key sentence:** Non-dual awareness is not the end of the contemplative journey but its beginning — the recognition of what was never absent, only obscured by the systems that taught us to see ourselves as fundamentally separate.

---

## Ethics: The queer dissolution of fixed identity

Patrick Cheng's queer theology argues that the dissolution of fixed identity boundaries is one of queerness's most significant contributions to contemplative life. The queer experience of not fitting fixed categories — of being neither/both, of living in the between — can be a form of preparation for the non-dual recognition. The second-satan system demands fixed categories precisely because they are the units of control; non-dual awareness dissolves the fixity that control requires.

**Key sentence:** Queer experience of inhabiting the between — neither/both, uncategorizable — is not a problem to be resolved but a preparation for non-dual recognition, and this is queerness's most underacknowledged contemplative gift.

---

## Emergence: Not a state to achieve but what is always already present

The art practice that most naturally opens onto non-dual awareness is the one in which the maker's separation from the material gradually dissolves — where the mark-making body and the receiving surface become one process. Flow states, as Csikszentmihalyi describes them, are moments of this dissolution: the effortless, absorbed engagement in which the boundary between self and activity temporarily relaxes. The studio is a technology for inducing these moments, and they are not separate from the deepest contemplative states.

**Key sentence:** The absorption that genuine art-making produces — where the maker and the made are momentarily undivided — is non-dual awareness in practice, and the studio is one of its most reliable entry points.

## Art Practice Invitation

Make something without stopping to evaluate it. Set a timer for forty minutes and do not step back, do not assess, do not correct. Stay in contact with the material without interruption. Notice what happens to the sense of a separate self doing the making.
