# Week 03 · The window of tolerance is not fixed — it is a scar left by every system that required you to be more than you could hold

**Phase:** I — The Body's Wisdom  
**Constellation:** The Body's Wisdom  
**Star:** Window of tolerance

---

## Grounding: The zone of integration

Daniel Siegel's concept of the window of tolerance names the zone of arousal within which integration is possible — where the body is activated enough to feel, but regulated enough to process. Below the window: numbness, collapse, dissociation. Above it: overwhelm, flooding, the shutdown of reflective capacity. Contemplative practice, somatic therapy, and creative work all require some access to this window. None of them can be forced; they can only be inhabited when the body is sufficiently resourced.

**Key sentence:** Every contemplative, somatic, and creative practice worth its name works within the window of tolerance — and the first task is always to find where that window currently is, not where we wish it were.

---

## Ethics: Whose window has been narrowed, and by what?

The window of tolerance is not equally distributed. Chronic exposure to systems that police, reject, or extract — religious institutions that condemn queerness, medical systems that pathologize trans bodies, family systems that replicate institutional cruelty — systematically narrows the window over time. The person who cannot sustain contemplative practice is not spiritually deficient; they may be carrying a narrowed window as the accurate record of what they have survived. Spiritual direction that ignores this is not neutral — it is a further demand on an already-depleted body.

**Key sentence:** A narrow window of tolerance is not a personal failure; it is the body's honest record of what it has been asked to carry — and any practice that treats it as an obstacle rather than a starting point has not yet understood what it is doing.

---

## Emergence: Widening as contemplative practice

The window of tolerance can be widened. Gradually, through somatic practice, through safety in community, through the repetition of repair after rupture, through contemplative practice approached with genuine care for the body's actual state. This widening is the spiritual journey at its most literal and most material — the expansion of what the body can be present to. Art-making is one of the most reliable ways into this widening: the studio offers containment, rhythm, sensory engagement, and the possibility of absorption that does not demand performance.

**Key sentence:** The spiritual life, at its most honest, is the slow expansion of the window — learning to be present to more and more of what is, without fleeing into either numbness or overwhelm.

## Art Practice Invitation

Make something from within your actual window — not from where you wish you were, but from where you are. If the window is currently narrow, let that be the material. A narrow mark. A contracted form. Constraint as honesty.
