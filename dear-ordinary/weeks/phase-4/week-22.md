# Week 22 · The Earth is not a backdrop to the spiritual life — it is a theological partner in it

**Phase:** IV — The Engaged Path
**Constellation:** The Wide Waters
**Star:** Earth as theological partner

---

## Grounding: Symbiosis not competition

Thomas Berry's *The Dream of the Earth* and Lynn Margulis's theory of symbiogenesis — the discovery that eukaryotic cells arose through symbiosis between bacteria, not competition — establish the same claim from theology and biology respectively: life is inherently cooperative, mutual, and relational at its most fundamental level. Forests are networks of mutual dependency; Sheldrake's work on mycelial networks shows that what appears to be individual trees are participants in a vast communication system. The universe is a communion of subjects, as Berry puts it — not a collection of objects to be used.

**Key sentence:** The Earth is not a resource for the spiritual life to consume — it is a subject in the contemplative encounter, a teacher, a partner, and its destruction is not merely an environmental problem but a theological one.

---

## Ethics: The same logic that hoards power over queer bodies justifies the domination of Earth

Queer ecology — a field developed by Catriona Sandilands and others — names what has been visible from multiple angles: the same conceptual system that naturalizes the domination of queer bodies also naturalizes the domination of Earth. Heteronormativity and ecological domination are not parallel problems with separate solutions; they are the same problem operating in different domains. The second-satan logic that hoards power through the policing of bodies is the same logic that treats the Earth as available for extraction.

**Key sentence:** Queer ecology's insight — that the logic of heteronormativity and the logic of ecological domination are the same logic — means that the contemplative work of queering is already ecological work, and ecological healing is already queer healing.

---

## Emergence: The studio as ecological relationship

The materials of art practice are materials of the Earth: pigment from mineral and plant, paper from wood fiber, clay from riverbed, charcoal from burned wood. Attending to the materiality of art-making — genuinely sensing the particular qualities of the material being used — is a form of ecological relationship. It is the opposite of treating materials as interchangeable units of production. The studio, when it is genuinely attentive, is a practice of the I-Thou encounter with the non-human world.

**Key sentence:** Attending to the particular materiality of art-making — the specific weight of this clay, the grain of this paper, the way this pigment moves — is a form of ecological contemplation, a practice of recognizing the Earth as a subject rather than a resource.
