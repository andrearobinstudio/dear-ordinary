# Week 38 · Art-making reaches forms of experience that language cannot access — because some experience is stored in image and sensation, not in word

**Phase:** VI — The Wide Waters / The Making
**Constellation:** The Making
**Star:** The pre-verbal path

---

## Grounding: What language cannot reach

Steven Levine's *Poiesis* and James Pennebaker's expressive writing research together establish the claim: art-making reaches forms of experience that language cannot access, not because language is insufficient but because some experience is stored in image, movement, and sensation rather than word. The trauma that is held in the body as posture, tension, and startle response is not easily translated into narrative; but it may be accessible to the hand holding charcoal, the body moving through space, the eye attending to color and form. Girija Kaimal's research shows that art-making reduces cortisol regardless of prior artistic experience — the benefit is not contingent on skill.

**Key sentence:** Art-making reaches what language cannot because some experience is stored in the body's non-verbal systems — and this means the studio is not a supplement to the talking cure but a different and sometimes more direct route to what is held.

---

## Ethics: Who gets labeled as preliterate or inarticulate

The privileging of verbal intelligence — of the capacity to articulate experience in language — has operated as a form of second-satan gatekeeping in both clinical and educational contexts. People who process experience more through image, movement, body, and sensation than through language have been labeled preliterate, inarticulate, or unintelligent — when in fact they may be using forms of intelligence that language-centered systems cannot measure. Expressive arts therapy's insistence on the validity of non-verbal processing is not merely therapeutic; it is political.

**Key sentence:** The privileging of verbal intelligence over embodied and imagistic intelligence is not a neutral educational preference — it is a gatekeeping mechanism that excludes forms of knowing that cannot be captured in the language the system recognizes.

---

## Emergence: The studio as the site of pre-verbal truth

Cathy Malchiodi's handbook of art therapy and its clinical evidence base, combined with the contemplative tradition's understanding of apophatic knowing, establish the studio's unique capacity: to hold what cannot yet be said. This is not a lesser form of knowing that will eventually be translated into language; it is its own form of knowing, with its own validity, its own depth, and its own relationship to what is true. The art practice that honors this — that does not rush toward interpretation and language — is practicing the via negativa applied to image.

**Key sentence:** The art practice that does not rush toward interpretation — that allows the image to remain in its own form rather than being translated into language — is practicing the via negativa applied to making, and honoring the particular validity of imagistic knowing.

## Art Practice Invitation

Make something about an experience you have never been able to put into words. Do not try to illustrate it — let the body find the form. Do not translate it afterward. Let it remain in the image.
